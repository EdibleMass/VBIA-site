The Great Canadian Giving Challenge

Each dollar donated gives Vancouver Brain Injury Association a chance to win $20,000.

People suffering from acquired brain injuries face challenges on a daily basis, and this is only worsened by the COVID-19 pandemic. Although VBIA in-person activities have been halted due to COVID-19, we hope to continue offering our services online. By donating, you are providing the VBIA with much-needed resources to reach out and help our clients in these especially difficult times.

How Will VBIA Use the $20,000 if We Win?

Investing in Outreach Response and Support

Along with reinforcing existing VBIA advocacy, support group, and rehabilitation services, we will use this prize money to hire an Occupational Therapist (OT). There are currently no on-call outreach workers in Metro Vancouver for people affected by brain injury. By hiring an OT, we will be able to respond to the emergent needs of people affected by brain injury in a timely manner.

Living With Brain Injury During a Global Pandemic

Confused. Anxious. Isolated. Depressed.


Imagine yourself suffering from a brain injury. Throughout the day, you are often feeling confused, anxious, depressed, and isolated. Now, add to that the hardships faced during the COVID-19 pandemic, and you can imagine how overwhelming that would feel. This is precisely why your donations are crucial - now more than ever - to improve the lives of brain injury survivors. We understand that you may be facing economic uncertainty, nonetheless, we ask you to consider extending a gesture of kindness to your community and supporting our cause.
